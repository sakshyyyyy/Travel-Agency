St Paul's Cathedral is the most famous and iconic cathedral in London. It has been used as a backdrop for many major events throughout history, including the wedding of King Charles III to Lady Diana Spencer and Winston Churchill's funeral.

The SEA LIFE London Aquarium is a popular marine attraction on London's South Bank, featuring over 500 species, themed zones like Shark Walk and Penguin Point, and interactive exhibits. It’s family-friendly and focuses on marine conservation.

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell inside the Elizabeth Tower at the Palace of Westminster in London. It also refers to the iconic clock and tower, completed in 1859. Known for its accuracy and famous chimes, it symbolizes the UK and is a major tourist attraction.

Westminster Abbey is a historic Gothic church in London, known for royal coronations, weddings, funerals, and as the burial site of monarchs and notable figures. It’s a key symbol of British heritage.

The Victoria and Albert Museum (V&A) in London is the world's largest museum of decorative arts and design. It houses over 2.3 million objects, including fashion, ceramics, jewelry, furniture, and art, spanning 5,000 years of history. Located in South Kensington, it’s renowned for its diverse and stunning collections.
