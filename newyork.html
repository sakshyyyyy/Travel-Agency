CARNEIGE HALL

Carnegie Hall is a world-renowned concert venue in New York City, known for its exceptional acoustics and prestigious history. Opened in 1891, it hosts a wide range of performances, from classical music to jazz and pop.

STATUE OF LIBERTY

The Statue of Liberty, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, is a symbol of freedom and democracy. Gifted by France in 1886, it stands as a welcoming beacon to immigrants arriving in the United States. The statue is a renowned landmark and national monument.

TIME SQUARE

Times Square is a bustling commercial and entertainment hub in the heart of Manhattan, known for its bright neon lights and massive digital billboards. It is a major tourist destination and the site of the annual New Year's Eve ball drop celebration.

METEROPOLITAN

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, located in New York City, is one of the largest and most prestigious art museums in the world. It houses an extensive collection spanning 5,000 years of art, from ancient civilizations to modern works. The museum is a major cultural landmark and destination for art lovers.

BRYANT PARK

Bryant Park is a scenic public park located in the heart of Manhattan, offering green space, seasonal activities, and outdoor events. It features a large lawn, a skating rink in winter, and is surrounded by iconic skyscrapers, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists.
